# Data Sources — Mining & Rare Earth Monte Carlo Simulation

Channel parameters in `mc_simulation.py` trace to the sources listed here.
The paper (Mining & Rare Earth: Ore Grade Depletion Floor) is available on SSRN and contains the full
reference list and §4 calibration narrative.

---

## Channel Sources

### `C1_environmental_degradation`

Radioactive tailings, acid mine drainage, deforestation, biodiversity loss, water contamination. Bayan Obo: 70km² tailings lake. 35Mt hazardous waste/yr globally. Sources: Nuss & Eckelman 2014, Ali 2014, UNEP IRP.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*

### `C2_labor_health`

Silicosis, heavy-metal poisoning, artisanal mining deaths (40K/yr DRC cobalt alone), child labor 40K+. Sources: ILO, Amnesty International, Lancet.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*

### `C3_community_fpic`

Forced displacement (10M+ people), FPIC violations, indigenous land seizure, cultural destruction. Sources: Oxfam, First Peoples Worldwide, ICMM.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*

### `C4_perpetual_remediation`

Post-closure liability extends centuries. 500K+ abandoned mines in US alone. Acid drainage treatment in perpetuity. Sources: EPA, ICMM, Fraser Institute.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*

### `C5_geopolitical_concentration`

China controls 60-90% of REE processing. Supply weaponization (2010 Japan embargo). Strategic vulnerability premium. Sources: USGS, EU Critical Raw Materials Act, IEA.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*

### `C6_governance_resource_curse`

Dutch disease, institutional erosion, conflict minerals ($185M/yr to armed groups), transparency failures. Sources: Sachs & Warner, EITI, Global Witness, OECD Due Diligence.

*Full citations: paper §4 (available SSRN).*
